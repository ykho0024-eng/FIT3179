Personal Safety in Australia

Understanding Violence Patterns Across States and Demographics

Personal safety remains a fundamental aspect of wellbeing, reflecting how
secure people feel in their daily lives and relationships. This
visual narrative explores trends in physical and sexual violence
in Australia, using national survey and crime data from the
Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS). Through a series of visualisations,
we uncover how experiences of personal violence have evolved over
time, vary across states, and differ by gender, relationship, and
demographic factors.

National Overview

This section provides a broad picture of personal safety across Australia. It highlights total reported offences, victimization rates, and national trends over time, helping establish the scale and direction of personal safety concerns.

Population-adjusted rates mirror total cases, indicating real behavioral shifts rather than demographic changes.

National data shows trends that form the foundation for understanding Australia's safety landscape. To understand where these offences are most frequent, we next compare offence cases by type and state.

Gender and Age Patterns

This section explores how gender and age influence victimization rates and offence patterns, revealing which groups are most at risk.

  • Teenage girls face highest sexual assault risk
  • Robbery peaks in teenage years for both genders
  • Females disproportionately affected by sexual assault across all age groups.

These patterns show that personal safety risks differ sharply across demographics, highlighting the need for targeted support. Next, we examine how weapon involvement shapes offence severity.

Weapons Used

Weapon use adds another dimension to understanding personal safety, indicating not only the presence of violence but its potential severity.

  • 91% of assaults and 99% of sexual assaults involve no weapons.
  • Robbery is most weapon-dependent (45% of cases)
  • Knives are the most common weapon type
  • Homicide shows greatest weapon variety
  • Firearms remain relatively rare

To further contextualize these incidents, we now look at where crimes most commonly occur.

Crime Location

Crime risk is strongly shaped by location. The heatmap shows a clear split between private and public settings:

  • Homes dominate for assault and sexual assault, highlighting domestic violence prevalence.
  • Robbery concentrates in streets, transport, and retail areas.
  • Transport hubs are robbery hotspots.
  • Community spaces show moderate assault levels.

These patterns reveal how routine environments influence vulnerability. Next, we examine one of the most revealing factors: the relationship between victim and perpetrator.
