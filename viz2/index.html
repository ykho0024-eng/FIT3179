Personal Safety in Australia

Understanding Violence Patterns Across States and Demographics

Personal safety remains a fundamental aspect of wellbeing, reflecting how secure people feel in their daily lives and relationships. This visual narrative explores trends in physical and sexual violence in Australia, using national survey and crime data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS). Through a series of visualisations, we uncover how experiences of personal violence have evolved over time, vary across states, and differ by gender, relationship, and demographic factors.

National Trends Over Time

Three distinct phases emerge in Australia's personal safety landscape:

2008-2013: Significant decline in offences, likely due to improved policing and legislation.
2013-2019: Relative stability with minor fluctuations.
Post-2019: Sharp increase, potentially linked to COVID impacts and reporting changes.

Population-adjusted rates mirror total cases, indicating real behavioral shifts rather than demographic changes.

State Comparison

Dramatic variations exist across Australian jurisdictions:

  • Northern Territory: Consistently highest rates (>450 per 10,000)
  • Queensland: Apparent spike reflects data reporting changes, not actual crime surge
  • Western Australia: Steady deterioration with rates nearly doubling over 25 years
  • Victoria: Lowest-risk state with stable performance
  • ACT: Notable improvement since 2007 peak

Offence Type Composition

Assault dominates, comprising 70-80% of all offences throughout the timeline.

Steady growth from mid-1990s to mid-2000s was followed by a dip around 2008-2012, suggesting improved prevention systems.

Sexual assault shows clear increase post-2015, likely reflecting greater reporting confidence rather than more incidents.

Homicide and kidnapping remain consistently small proportions of total offences.

Gender and Age Patterns

Clear demographic patterns emerge in victimization:

  • Teenage girls face highest sexual assault risk
  • Adults 25-44 experience greatest assault burden
  • Males dominate physical violence offences
  • Females disproportionately affected by sexual assault
  • Robbery peaks in teenage years for both genders
Women aged 18-34 record the highest total offence counts across categories.

Crime Locations

Clear divide between private and public crime environments:

Homes dominate for assault and sexual assault, highlighting domestic violence prevalence.

Public spaces see different patterns:

  • Robbery concentrates in streets, transport, and retail areas
  • Community spaces show moderate assault levels
  • Transport hubs are robbery hotspots

Weapon Use in Crimes

Most violent crimes occur without weapons:

91% of assaults and 99% of sexual assaults involve no weapons.

When weapons are used:

  • Robbery is most weapon-dependent (45% of cases)
  • Knives are the most common weapon type
  • Homicide shows greatest weapon variety
  • Firearms remain relatively rare
