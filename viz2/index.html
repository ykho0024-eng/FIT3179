Personal Safety in Australia

Understanding Violence Patterns Across States and Demographics

Personal safety remains a fundamental aspect of wellbeing, reflecting how secure people feel in their daily lives and relationships. This visual narrative explores trends in physical and sexual violence in Australia, using national survey and crime data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS). Through a series of visualisations, we uncover how experiences of personal violence have evolved over time, vary across states, and differ by gender, relationship, and demographic factors.

National Overview — How Has Personal Safety Changed Over Time?

Trends in total violence prevalence from 1993 to 2024.

We begin with a national perspective. This visualisation shows how the prevalence of total violence has shifted between 1993 and 2024, providing a broad overview of the changing landscape of personal safety in Australia.

Offence Type Composition from 1995-2024

Breakdown of physical offences by type over time.

Due to unpublished data of assault in some states and years, physical offences are grouped as Assault and Other Physical Offences (homicide, kidnapping) for data consistency. This visualization shows how the composition of different offence types has evolved over nearly three decades.

State Comparison: Geographic Variation

Physical offence rates across Australian states and territories.

The map uses normalized color scaling to enable visual comparison across different offence types. Each crime category is converted to a common 0-10 scale based on expected ranges: Total Physical Offence (0-15 per 100,000), Assault (400-1500 per 100,000), and Sexual Assault (30-150 per 100,000). The formula (Actual_Rate - Min_Range) / (Max_Range - Min_Range) * 10 transforms all values to this consistent scale.

Weapon Choice Across Criminal Offences

Relationships between weapon types and offence categories.

This Sankey diagram illustrates the relationship between weapon types and offence categories, revealing patterns in how different weapons are associated with various crimes. The flow visualization clearly shows the distribution and prevalence of weapons across offence types, with thicker streams indicating more frequent combinations. By tracing the connections from left to right, you can observe which weapons are most commonly involved in specific offences, providing valuable insights into the nature of violent crimes and the tools typically employed in different criminal contexts.

Gender Comparison — Differences in Experiences

Age and gender distribution of victims across offence types.

This population pyramid compares men's and women's experiences of physical and sexual assault by age group. The visualization reveals distinct patterns in victim demographics, showing how vulnerability varies across different stages of life and between genders.
