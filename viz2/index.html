Personal Safety in Australia

Understanding Violence Patterns Across States and Demographics

Personal safety remains a fundamental aspect of wellbeing, reflecting how secure people feel in their daily lives and relationships. This visual narrative explores trends in physical and sexual violence in Australia, using national survey and crime data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS). Through a series of visualisations, we uncover how experiences of personal violence have evolved over time, vary across states, and differ by gender, relationship, and demographic factors.

State Comparison: Geographic Variation

Physical offence rates across Australian states and territories.

The map uses normalized color scaling to enable visual comparison across different offence types. Each crime category is converted to a common 0-10 scale based on expected ranges: Total Physical Offence (0-15 per 100,000), Assault (400-1500 per 100,000), and Sexual Assault (30-150 per 100,000). The formula (Actual_Rate - Min_Range) / (Max_Range - Min_Range) * 10 transforms all values to this consistent scale.
